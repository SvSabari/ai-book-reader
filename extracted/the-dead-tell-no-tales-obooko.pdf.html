SYNOPSIS “The Dead Tell No Tales” Is a reflective poetry collection that meditates on silence, memory, and the limitations of wisdom after death. Kenneth Oodee explores the tension between humanity’s desperate search for truth and the finality of the grave, revealing how secrets, regrets, and unspoken words are buried and alongside the dead. Through haunting imagery and philosophical inquiry, the collection questions the value of wisdom that arrives too late to be lived, if only there were a second chance, but there isn’t.

MEDITATIONS FROM THE GRAVEYARD People never care until you are resting in peace or in pieces Their love is creamed, clothed in greed and pretense We have always known, yet the hypocrisy was comforting like hoping for true love while knowing they are only flirting People come and go Most times, it is how they leave that lives forever We receive thousands of roses in death, As if we were gardeners Yet alive, not even a single rose Were we granted to hold Life is a course Humans are the curse There is nothing loving In a resentful world, Nothing melodious In a hateful word No wonder the truest life Is the life after life These are the thoughts and meditations from the graveyard.

THE ILLUSION OF OWNERSHIP They never grow tired of sleeping They rest, and by extension, rest on Some in peace, some in chaos It all depends on the life before Some are happy, some are sad It all depends on the destination after Their beds are beautifully furnished Yet some lived wretched in poverty They are, by far, the wealthiest Yet many lived lives of abject scarcity How do we explain those who died at twenty Yet were not buried until forty? How do we explain those who owned a hundred houses But were laid in a box smaller than half a home? This is the mystery reality never tells us The world is ours Yet in truth, we owned nothing.

THE ECONOMY OF THE DEAD The graveyard runs out of room For the dead hold thousands of secrets The world is filled with riches Yet the graveyard is filled with wealth Talents buried into eternity Birds perch upon every tomb Cobwebs curtain forgotten glory Where time stands still, yet ticks consume Treasures fine, beyond all measure No beauty born of gold or sight An invisible crowd in silent motion Walking through eternal night Some arrive humble, unnamed by history Some crowned by legacy and pride Endless tales, forever untold For dead men take their stories inside.

THE MERCY OF THE GRAVE On the road to the cemetery lies scattered pain, Nothing to wash it away, not even the rain In the graveyard rests a man, still alive Striving with every breath he has left to survive Clothed in misery and crowned by death To others, not a single wound was felt For he lived a life of unfiltered wickedness Killing and stealing, driven by blind ambitiousness Greed and covetousness were his idols Without conscience, he committed the abominable Bad intentions, followed by worse decisions Evil became both his fame and addiction To save humanity in moments of urgency Sometimes, even death is mercy.

OH DEATH! Oh death The bitter taste of life Your hunger, born of desperation never rests. You are the end of living Yet from the very beginning You trouble us like a restless wife Oh death You are never satisfied Your fleeting desires never quenches Either you have killed, Or you are killing, Or you are already on your way. No holidays No remorse Not even a moment of chill Oh death The Sinner’s Nightmare You circle your victims Like a lover in a secret affair You are full of rage And emptied of mercy Blooming in tragedy Without apology Oh death Sometimes you are selective in your wickedness

The innocent remain Your easiest victims You justify yourself without morality, and in doing so become your own abomination. Yours Truly

THE HEIGHT BELOW They have their opinions They have their suggestions They have their pain And yes, their stories too They scream the loudest, yet are never heard In the sphere of time, their invisibility rhymes So many wise words, dismissed as foolish by the living For even chaff is deemed more valuable by comparison Their stories are braided with pain and glory Mixed with shame and folly alike This is the highest height their wisdom can attain Beneath us lies the peak of their remaining fame Their beauty shines only in darkness For the dead hold no growth.

FOOTPRINTS OF REVERENCE The echoes of an audible silence Escorted by a solemn ambience A cemetery clothed in incense The ground littered with condolences Crested upon the tombs are epitaphs Somehow, we all earn our autographs At last, life becomes fair Even when, while she lived We never shared a good affair The evening wind drops rusty leaves And on that red carpet linger ancient beliefs “Respect for ancestors” Words not borrowed from poet or author This is spiritual and cultural admiration A footprint passed down From generation to generation.

AFFECTION AFTER DEATH Some call it the graveyard, Some call it a garden of roses Endless gifts of flowers One way or another, Its occupants were once lovers For romance has no limits Neither does remembrance keep a date We are inspired by memories Compelled by the legacies left behind Some we loved, and some we hated It does not matter now; they are late For sometimes death breeds love And sometimes the lives we lived inspire hate Even though we often care too late Why must we truly deserve flowers Only in death?

WHAT THE DEAD REMEMBER They no longer remember their enemies Nor do they recall their favorite pursuits Only their legacies and how they lived Their destination, and what they failed to become The pain of unlived lives The fear of what the world might have said The kindness they could not extend The love and generosity withheld The world is overly congested Yet hollow of truth and originality Everything is an illusion Even the sanest mind is void of true knowledge.

THE BOUNDARY OF TIME As the night turned black, The world returned Back to before creation, When reality existed without form The dead littered the streets The wind grew strict with shivers Echoes rose from whispered creatures Yet nature’s realm remained orderly Covered in fog stood the cemetery One after another, they stepped out Like a pageantry of the unseen It was their time to sell and trade Their time to run the economy They live outside of time Yet the boundaries of life Remain governed by it.

THEY LIVE AHEAD There is no electricity, yet they see ahead No banks to save in, yet their treasures are never lost No good roads, yet no accidents occur No markets stand, yet trade moves in order No schools, still wisdom and knowledge flourish No church, yet their bones dangle in praise and worship No offices, no leaders, yet they work, respecting hierarchy No hospitals exist, for their health is eternal Their faith walks ahead of deeds Their fate stretches beyond their lives in the dark They sleep within the tomb, yet live in the afterlife Like humans, they do not live in the present they live in the future Their home has no walls even when their boxes limit their shadows on the floor.

THE CURRICULUM OF DEATH The entire knowledge of life Lies six feet below Human knowledge is foolishness Corrupted and bloated with pride “The dead tell no tales” Yet silence harbours wisdom The living despise silence Yet noise utters foolishness The ironies of life are alarming As the scales of oxymoron dangle through the night Written on the scroll of life Are stories that succeed death Written in the scroll of death Are stories that precede life.

GRAVES WITHOUT STONES Some are buried beneath the sea, Where mortal eyes can scarcely see Some are buried in the ground Where even rising waters cannot drown Some are buried in the forest, Hoping with their ancestors, they may rest Some are buried in the desert Where their treasures lie far from reach Some are buried in foreign lands, Far from familiar tongues and hands Some are buried in graveyards Known, named, and marked Yet there is no better place to be laid Than within the heart For the heart is a home Where we can truly live forever And by the way, This poem is not about death.

TEARS ON EVERY STONE The lonely cemetery whispers aloud, While noisy occupants lie silent, yet proud The hearts of immortals, no tears, no devotion, The minds of immortals, endless in motion Corpses drool upon ancient beds Tombstones serve as pillows for weary heads There is guilt in every brittle bone There are tears upon every stone Memories buried beneath regret Karma arrives to collect each debt The echoes rise from the graveyard deep The raven’s cry disturbs their sleep The value and worth of death remain The price and cost of life—our pain.

CANTICLE OF SHADOWS The mighty wind runs in circles, Ancient fallen leaves begin to rustle Bones stretch quietly on their beds The long, unbroken slumber of the dead Shadows linger on every stone Together in rest, yet still alone Whistling pines mourn in melody Pouring their grief into rhapsody Spirits linger, hovering around Gathering memories from the ground Twilight devours the dying light Crows and owls speak in turns, and that’s alright Every moment, every minute The dead reject a banquet of the living.

EQUAL DUST A yard filled with sin and righteousness, Blood and tears Wisdom and illiteracy Truth and lies Pain and happiness The rich and the poor The haves and the have-nots Beauty and boldness The humble and the proud The fearful and the confident The good and the bad The young and the old The blessed and the unfortunate White and Black Straight and gay Here stands the wealthiest place on Earth.

FROM WOMB TO TOMB From the womb, we are wrapped in darkness, Taught how to live, yet told to mind our business Given identities by names we never chose All we hear is, “It has been this way long ago.” We are called by ancient names of ancestors Yet told we are newborns, what a soothing lie. They say we are free to become whoever we want But when we do, we’re reckless, nonchalant The world itself feels like a trap, a prison You’re either an outcast or an obedient citizen Suffocating beneath the illusion of freedom Watched by cameras, bound by constitution Down to the tomb, we remain clothed in darkness There is no freedom in this world.

IN THE END “Let us tell the truth, shall we? That nothing truly lasts at all So, give more love than you acquire possessions For in the end, they all become ashes Trust nothing and no one to be your god There is nothing in life except life itself The anger that consumes you will eventually reduce you The pride that clothes you will also limit you Life is meaningless without purpose Purpose is void without its source Its source is the road to its Creator This is the only journey in life There is so much time, yet there is no time Some live in the morning, Some at noon, And some make it to the night. Yet only two things remain constant Regret and reward And, by extension, a little of both”?

ADIEU There are thousands of lonely graves, Filled with the foolish and the brave Covered in darkness, buried in lies Still starving for truth on the beds where they lie In life, they tasted pain and shame Moments they betrayed, and times they were blamed They were crucified for choosing the true And their only reward was a final adieu Some souls still suffer Night after night, their spirits cry out Till eternity, some wonder what death was about Their blood flowing endlessly through corridors of time “Gone too soon” has learned their names, turned grief into rhyme Some souls are too gentle to survive the living Some souls die too often in their search for life.

SPEAK NO EVIL OF THE DEAD In reverence, speak no evil, Neither in remembrance, let not our words be cruel The wind carries away our echoes Down to the earth where they decompose And time keeps her records for eternity The dead have no pressing necessity Just as the living have their messengers So too the dead, in hierarchy, have their messengers Do not speak ill of the dead Their troubles and peace have long been laid Their sins are buried beneath the dust of earth And in reminiscence, the grave holds no mirth So let our grace and mercy intercede, For the grave is soft, but judgment is harsh indeed.

THE DEAD TELL NO TALES “The dead tell no tales,” Neither do they reveal details Every secret rest in their legacies There lie their truths and their fallacies “The dead tell no tales,” Buried with every unspoken word This is one tragedy death entails One limitation of the graveyard “The dead tell no tales,” Yet humans are desperate to hear Like searching for a river in the desert Foolishness, indeed, inseparable from man “The dead tell no tales,” No wonder the cemetery is full of wisdom Perhaps the most useless form of wisdom of all For what is wisdom, if it cannot be applied?
